Class A vs. Class B uniforms

Class A: The full official uniform, sometimes called "Field uniform" (button-down shirt with patches, neckerchief and slide, belt, hat, pants/shorts.)

Recommended for: pack meetings, ceremonies, flag events, parades, council/district events, and any time we are representing Scouting in public.

Class B: Sometimes called "Activity uniform." A Scout T‑shirt (Pack or BSA) with Scout‑appropriate pants/shorts or skirt. Neckwear and hat are optional.

Recommended for: den meetings, outdoor activities, messy crafts, service projects, and active events where comfort and durability matter.

If you are ever unsure which uniform to wear, check the event description, or ask your Den Leader or Cubmaster.
When in doubt, wear Class A with Class B underneath.

Thrifty uniform tips

Buy big: Choose a shirt that’s a little large so your Scout can grow into it over multiple years.

Used and shared: Check with our Pack uniform closet, older Scouts, thrift stores, and online marketplaces for gently used shirts, pants, and hats.

Webelos transition: As scouts move into the Webelos rank, they may continue to wear their "blue" uniform pieces, or they may start wearing "tan/green" uniform pieces. Choose what makes the most sense for your finances and child's growth.

Focus on the essentials: Start with the items marked “Required” below; you can add “Recommended” and “Optional” pieces over time.
