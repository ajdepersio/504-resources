What Is Cub Scouts?

A fun, family-centered program that helps kids grow through adventure

THE PURPOSE OF CUB SCOUTS

Cub Scouts gives elementary-age kids a place to belong, try new things, and grow with support from caring adults. It is designed to be fun, age-appropriate, and family-centered so growth happens through shared experiences, not just classroom-style instruction.

  • Belonging: every child and family has a place in the pack
  • Discovery: Scouts try outdoors, STEM, service, and hands-on skills
  • Confidence: kids build independence by completing small challenges
  • Family connection: parents participate and grow alongside their Scout
THE AIMS OF SCOUTING

These four aims guide every adventure, meeting, and activity:

  • Character Development — learning to make good choices
  • Leadership Development — practicing responsibility and teamwork
  • Citizenship Training — understanding community and service
  • Personal Fitness — physical, mental, and emotional well-being
HOW THE PROGRAM WORKS

Cub Scouts are organized into dens by grade level, with each grade having its own rank: Lion, Tiger, Wolf, Bear, Webelos, and Arrow of Light for grades K-5 respectively.

Each rank completes short, age-appropriate, themed adventures that teach skills, teamwork, and values. Completing the 6 required and at least 2 elective adventures earns the rank badge.

WHAT KIDS DO IN CUB SCOUTS

National highlights these core experiences:

  • Outdoor activities — hikes, nature exploration, camping
  • STEM projects — building, experimenting, problem-solving
  • Community service — helping others and giving back
  • Games & teamwork — learning cooperation and leadership
  • Family involvement — parents participate alongside their Scout

HOW FAMILIES PARTICIPATE

Cub Scouts is a family program — parents and guardians are part of the experience. No prior experience is needed; any family can participate.

  • Attend meetings
  • Support adventure activities
  • Help with simple tasks
  • Encourage their Scout
WHAT DOES CUB SCOUTS COST?

Pack 504 keeps Scouting affordable for families. We do not charge any pack-level dues up front — your only required annual costs are the national and council fees.

  • National Membership Fee: $85 — paid once per year to Scouting America
  • Crossroads of America Council Fee: $80 — supports local camps, programs, and insurance
  • Pack 504 Unit Fee — none (we do not collect pack dues)

Optional activities such as campouts and special events may have per‑event fees. We work hard to keep these low and accessible for all families.

Financial assistance is available for families who need it. Cost is never be a barrier to participation.
