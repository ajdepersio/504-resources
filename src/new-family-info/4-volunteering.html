Volunteering in Pack 504

Small roles. Big impact. No Scouting experience required.

THE PROGRAM CANNOT RUN WITHOUT YOU

Cub Scouts is entirely volunteer-led. There are no paid staff at the pack level — every meeting, campout, ceremony, and adventure happens because a parent showed up. Without adult volunteers, there is no program.

Every registered adult volunteer completes Safeguarding Youth Training (SYT), a free, online course required by Scouting America in addition to their position-specific training. Many volunteers also complete optional training such as BALOO, University of Scouting, and Wood Badge. You will never be asked to work with kids alone — two-deep leadership is always required and always provided.

WHY ADULTS VOLUNTEER

Cub Scouts isn't just great for kids — it's great for adults too. You'll gain:

  • Connection — meet other families and build community
  • Confidence — learn new skills in a supportive environment
  • Shared experiences — make memories with your child
  • Leadership growth — practice communication and teamwork
  • Impact — your involvement directly strengthens the pack
HOW VOLUNTEERING WORKS

Pack 504 uses a “small jobs, big impact” approach — choose a role that fits your time and comfort level.

  • Short-term roles — one-off tasks or projects
  • Event-based roles — seasonal or event-specific roles
  • Year-round roles — registered volunteer positions

You can start small and grow if you want — or stay small. Every bit helps.

LEADERSHIP ROLES (YEAR-ROUND)
Cubmaster
Leads pack meetings, supports den leaders, and sets the tone for the program.
Assistant Cubmaster
Helps run pack meetings, ceremonies, and events.
Den Leader
Leads a grade-level den using simple, ready-made adventure plans.
Assistant Den Leader
Supports the den leader and helps with activities.
Committee Chair
Helps organize the adult side of the pack — planning, communication, and support.
Pack Committee Member
Light administrative roles: planning, communication, advancement, or logistics.
EVENT & ACTIVITY ROLES (SEASONAL)
Pinewood Derby Team
Track setup, weigh-in, and race support
Blue & Gold Banquet Team
Decorations, food, and setup
Outdoors Chair
Helps plan campouts and outdoor activities
Service Project Lead
Organizes service activities
New Member Chair
Welcomes and supports new families as they join the pack
Pack Photographer
Takes photos at events
Advancement Chair
Manages rank advancements and awards
MICRO-ROLES (SHORT-TERM HELPERS)
Bring Snacks
No prep needed — just show up with treats
Run a Game
Lead a simple activity at a pack meeting
Check-In Helper
Welcome families as they arrive at an event
Craft Station
Assist Scouts with a hands-on project
Setup & Teardown
Help arrange and clean up event spaces
Skit or Song
Lead a fun moment with the whole pack
NO EXPERIENCE REQUIRED

Most parents who volunteer in Cub Scouts have never been in Scouting before. We provide simple training, step-by-step guides, and a supportive team.

If you can show up and care about kids, you can volunteer.
